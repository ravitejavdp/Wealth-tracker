Privacy & Security

How Data is Handled

Your financial data is stored locally in your browser's storage. It is never transmitted to any third-party server except when you explicitly use the Google Drive sync feature.

Google Drive Security

The app uses the drive.file scope. This is the most secure access level: the app can only see and modify the specific file it creates (wealth_data.json). It cannot see your other Drive documents.

Understanding Tabs & Features

1. Inventory (The Database)

This is where you manage your accounts. The workflow is two-step:

  • Create a Container: Add the name of the account (e.g., "Vanguard", "HDFC Bank") and its category.
  • Add Snapshots: Click on a container to enter your monthly balance. Use "Clone Latest" to quickly copy the previous month's numbers and update only what changed.
  • Stock Category: Only assets marked as "Stock Market" are used in the FIRE and Stock Projections calculators.

2. Totals (The Dashboard)

A consolidated view of your wealth across India and USA. The Net Worth History table automatically calculates the "Periodic Change" (how much you grew month-over-month) and handles currency conversion using your set exchange rate.

3. Stock Projections

Visualizes the power of compounding. It takes your current Stock Market holdings and projects future growth based on an annual return rate and planned monthly SIPs.

4. SIP Planning

A "Reverse Calculator". Instead of asking what you'll have, you tell the app what you need (e.g., ₹1 Crore for a house) and the date you need it. The app calculates exactly how much you must invest monthly starting today.

5. FIRE Planning

Calculates when you can reach Financial Independence. It uses your Annual Expenses and Safe Withdrawal Rate (usually 4%) to find your target "Corpus". It factors in inflation to ensure your retirement date is realistic.

100% Client-Side

This application is a "Static Single Page App". It has no backend server. Your financial data never touches our servers because there are none.

Direct Drive Sync

When you connect Google Drive, the app communicates directly with Google's APIs. We use the drive.file scope, meaning the app can only see files it created.

Google Drive Setup Guide

To enable cloud syncing, you need to create your own Google Cloud project. This ensures you own the integration entirely.

1

Create a Google Cloud Project

Go to the Google Cloud Console. Click "Select a project" > "New Project". Give it a name like "MyWealthTracker".

2

Enable Google Drive API

In the sidebar, go to "APIs & Services" > "Library". Search for "Google Drive API" and click "Enable".

3

Configure OAuth Consent Screen

Go to "APIs & Services" > "OAuth consent screen".

  • Choose "External" User Type.
  • Fill in App Name and your Email.
  • In "Scopes", add .../auth/drive.file.
  • Add your own email as a "Test User" (required while in Testing mode).
4

Create Credentials

Go to "APIs & Services" > "Credentials".

  • API Key: Click "Create Credentials" > "API Key". Copy it.
  • OAuth Client ID: Click "Create Credentials" > "OAuth client ID". Select "Web application".
  • Authorized JavaScript origins: Add http://localhost and the URL where you host this file.
5

Connect in App

Go to the Settings tab in WealthTracker. Paste your API Key and Client ID, click "Update", and then "Connect Google Drive".
